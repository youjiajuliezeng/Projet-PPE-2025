Algiers revives its dream of a Hollywood epic for Emir Abd El-Kader

   After several failed attempts, a film is set to be dedicated to this
   hero of resistance against French colonial conquest, with the active
   support of President Abdelmadjid Tebboune.

   By Mustapha Kessous

   Published on December 27, 2025, at 6:25 pm (Paris)

   2 min read

   Emir Abd El-Kader, in 1865 Emir Abd El-Kader, in 1865 Emir Abd
   El-Kader, in 1865 WIKIPEDIA

   Emir Abd El-Kader (1808-1883) lived a thousand lives, including forging
   a national destiny that Algiers has been trying to bring to the big
   screen since the 1970s. During a meeting on December 8, President
   Abdelmadjid Tebboune revived the project to produce a feature film on
   the extraordinary life of the man known as the "greatest enemy of
   France."

   This decision carries strong political symbolism, as relations between
   Paris and Algiers have entered a period of unprecedented tension since
   Algeria's independence. The latest episode in this fraught relationship
   unfolded on December 24, when Algerian lawmakers passed a law
   criminalizing colonization. France promptly denounced this as an
   "obviously hostile initiative."

   Read more Subscribers only Algeria demands 'apologies' and
   'reparations' from France for its colonial past

   A political and military genius, a learned strategist respected even by
   his fiercest adversaries, El-Kader was the iconic leader of Algerian
   resistance to the French conquest in the 19^th century. For 15 years,
   he outmaneuvered one of the most powerful armies of his era, until his
   surrender in 1847. While in exile in Damascus, he took up arms to save
   the lives of several thousand Christians threatened by persecution from
   the Druze. This act earned him worldwide recognition, from Pope Pius IX
   to American President Abraham Lincoln. His humanism was celebrated in
   lengthy poems by Victor Hugo and Arthur Rimbaud.
