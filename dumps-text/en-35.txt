   I almost always seem to be anxious about something in them. I'm either
   late for class, or I forgot to feed my cat, or I can't find something I
   really need... There's always something wrong or amiss, and it's
   usually my fault. lol. Last night I dreamt that there were many kitties
   running around inside a house (or some place... I'm not sure if it was
   a house), and I had to pick them up and place them somewhere, but

   making me feel distressed. Gosh, I have such random dreams. -_-

   Most of my dreams relate to things going on in my life, emotions I'm
   feeling, things I really want to have or to be true or think about
   frequently... sometimes fears and anxieties... usually nothing too
   spectacular. Sometimes the scenarios can be a little far-fetched,
   though usually still based in reality somehow.

   There have been several recurring dreams I've had in my lifetime. As a
   kid, I used to dream about being alone in my parents' car, behind the
   steering wheel, with the car rolling down the hill on the road we lived
   on, headed straight for our mailbox. I always knew I was going to
   crash, but there was nothing I could do to stop the car, no one who
   could help... so I just sat there, terrified. The dream never actually
   got to the point of the car crashing, though; I always woke up while it
   was coasting down the hill. If I recall correctly, I think sometimes
   (though not always) my dad would be standing at the end of our
   driveway, just watching, helpless to do anything. Mostly I had this
   dream between the ages of about four and eight or so.

   The second recurring dream is one that started in my mid-teens and
   which I still have in various forms today (though nowhere near as
   frequently as I used to have it). At 14-15 I developed an eating
   disorder (still with me today at nearly 28), and during the worst years
   of restricting and being extremely underweight, I used to dream at
   least several times a week about completely losing control with food.
   I'd somehow have forgotten in the dream that I had an eating disorder
   and I'd eat normally like I used to (or I'd overeat), and then I'd
   suddenly remember, "Oh shit! I'm not allowed to do this anymore!" Lame
   as it sounds, those were terrifying dreams. I'd get hysterical in them,
   crying and screaming and panicking over what I'd done, how I'd ruined
   everything, how I'd done the worst possible thing in the world that I
   could think of -- something I no longer permitted myself to do in real
   life. Many times I'd wake up in a panic, and even though I'd soon
   realise it had just been a dream, I'd have to check my body, running my
   hands over the familiar bones I could feel, lying on my side with my
   knees together and making sure my thighs didn't touch when I was in
   that position, recalling the last thing I'd had to eat and recounting
   every detail of it in my mind to make absolutely sure I hadn't actually
   eaten something I wasn't "allowed" to. I still sometimes have these
   dreams now, though usually they focus on outright bingeing on massive
   amounts of food (not just eating normally) and then forgetting that I
   was "supposed" to throw it all up... lame, I know. :mellow:
